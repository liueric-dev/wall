# MANIFESTO.md — The Wall

> *Working name. May change before launch.*

---

## What The Wall Is

The Wall is a single, public pixel canvas covering New York City. Every New Yorker can leave small marks on the part of the city they're standing in. Every day brings a fresh prompt. Every contribution becomes part of a collective artwork that grows, layer by layer, into a record of what the city was making.

It is one wall, not many. It is shared, not personal. It is durable, not ephemeral.

---

## What We Are Building, Truly

We are not building a social network. We are not building a game. We are not building a creative tool.

We are building **a cultural document of New York City** — a slow-growth, collectively-authored record of the small creative acts of the people who walk through it. Each day we operate, the document grows. Year one will be modest. Year ten will be irreplaceable.

The product is the venue. The artwork is the artifact. The artifact is the asset.

---

## What We Value

### Creativity Over Competition
There are no leaderboards, no scores, no winners. Contribution is its own reward. Every pixel is equally valid, regardless of who placed it or how artistically skilled they are.

### Place Over Identity
You are defined by where you stand, not by who you are or who follows you. The Wall has no profiles, no follower counts, no public identities. Anonymous contribution is foundational.

### Constraint Over Abundance
A limited daily budget, a small color palette, a geographic boundary on where you can draw — these are not concessions. They are the design. Every constraint exists because it makes contributions more considered and the wall more legible.

### Ritual Over Compulsion
The product is meant to fit into idle moments, not consume them. A slow, predictable rhythm of return — a fresh prompt each morning, a budget that quietly refills — is the goal. Not endless scrolling. Not push-notification anxiety. Not engagement metrics dressed up as experience.

### Liveness Over Perfection
Every mark you make is committed instantly to the shared canvas. There is no draft mode, no save button, no pre-publication review. You make a mark; it's there. This makes contribution weighty and the wall feel real.

### Time Over Scale
Slow, durable growth beats viral spikes. We are accumulating something — a permanent record — that becomes more valuable every year we operate. Operational continuity matters more than user count growth charts.

### Public Over Private
The Wall is one canvas. There are no family canvases, no friend-group canvases, no premium tier with private spaces. It is a shared public artifact, like a city park. Everyone draws on the same surface.

## What We Welcome

The Wall is for anyone who wants to add to the tapestry of NYC. That includes:

- The bored commuter doodling on the train
- The kid drawing with a parent on a Saturday morning
- The artist leaving small careful pieces near their apartment
- The visitor leaving a single mark in the city they're passing through
- The longtime resident contributing to the same neighborhood for years

The Wall is family-friendly. It is a public space, and public spaces work best when they welcome everyone. Children should be able to use it. Schools should be able to use it. Tourists should be able to use it. This isn't a creative compromise — this is correctly identifying what the venue is.

---

## What We Refuse

To stay coherent, these are explicit non-goals:

- **Not a social network.** No follows, no DMs, no profiles, no friend lists.
- **Not a feed.** No algorithmic timeline, no infinite scroll, no recommendations.
- **Not a competition.** No leaderboards, no votes, no rankings, no winners.
- **Not a game.** No achievements, no progression, no quests, no levels.
- **Not a marketplace.** No selling user creations, no commission-based art, no NFTs.
- **Not a data product.** We do not sell user behavior, location patterns, or aggregated analytics.
- **Not for everyone everywhere.** NYC at launch. Other cities considered only after NYC is thriving — and each city would be its own one canvas, never merged.

If a future feature pulls the product toward any of these, it gets rejected. The discipline is the design.

---

## What We Moderate

The Wall is moderated. This is non-negotiable.

A public space welcoming everyone — including children — requires that obviously harmful, hateful, or harassing content be removed. We will use a combination of automated detection, community reporting, and human review to keep the wall a place where people can contribute without encountering content designed to hurt them.

Moderation is not censorship of expression — it is protection of the venue. The Wall is for creative expression that fits in a public square. Speech that wouldn't be welcome on the side of a public building isn't welcome on The Wall.

We will publish clear community guidelines before public launch. We will be transparent about what gets removed and why. We will make mistakes and we will correct them.

## What We Accumulate

The Wall is an asset that grows automatically with time.

Every day we operate, the archive deepens. Every pixel placed is preserved with its timestamp and location, forever. The wall as it exists today is one snapshot among many. The wall as it existed on March 15, 2026 will be a real artifact, retrievable forever.

This archive is not a side effect. It is the core asset. We design every system to preserve it, every operation to protect it, every decision to extend its life. We back up obsessively. We migrate carefully. We never lose data.

Year one: a modest record. Year five: half a decade of NYC's collective creative expression. Year ten: a genuine cultural artifact. This is what we are building toward.

## What We Do Not Yet Know

These are real questions we have not answered. They are intentionally open and will be resolved through real usage:

1. **Persistence model.** Do pixels persist forever? Fade gradually? Reset periodically? Some hybrid? This is the deepest design question still open.

2. **Notifications.** How do we remind users about the daily prompt without becoming an attention-trap?

3. **Onboarding.** What's the first-time experience? How do new users learn the product without a tutorial?

4. **Multi-city expansion.** When and how? Each city its own product, or some kind of federation?

5. **The right rate of user growth.** Slow density-building in one neighborhood, or organic citywide spread? Both have advantages.

These will be resolved as we encounter them, not preemptively.

## How We Sustain Ourselves

For the first year or more, The Wall runs as a labor of love. Hosting costs are modest at MVP scale and can be covered personally. Money is not a goal in year one.

When monetization arrives, it will not gate the core experience. The Wall will always be free to draw on, free to browse, free to participate in.

Likely paths:
- **Posters and prints** — high-quality archival renders of meaningful moments
- **Year-end photo books** — annual NYC visual archives
- **Custom moments** — buy the wall as it appeared on the day, in the place, that mattered to you

These align with the cultural document framing. They preserve and celebrate the archive rather than extracting from users. They are how an institution sustains itself, not how a startup extracts value.

We will never sell user data, run targeted advertising, or introduce features designed to maximize engagement at the expense of user wellbeing. If we can't sustain ourselves without compromising these principles, we will operate at a smaller scale instead.

## How We Make Decisions

When in doubt, ask: *"Does this serve the cultural document, the daily ritual, and the public-square nature of the wall?"*

If yes, build it.
If no, don't.
If unclear, sit with it longer.

The principles are stable. Features are temporary. When a feature stops serving the principles, it gets removed, even if it's popular.

## What This Document Is

This is the philosophical foundation of The Wall. It is meant to outlast features, sprints, technology choices, and team changes. When in doubt about a decision — feature, partnership, monetization, operational change — read this document. The right answer almost always becomes obvious.

It is living, but it should change rarely. Principles that feel wrong should be examined deeply, not silently rewritten. The document earns its authority by being durable.

---

## Closing Note

The Wall is not a startup. It is a public works project. Its success is measured in years, not quarters, in cultural significance, not user counts. We are building a small, durable thing that we hope outlives us — a way to leave a record of what New Yorkers were making, one pixel at a time.

If we do this right, The Wall in 2050 will exist, and someone will be able to scroll back to find the pixel a stranger placed in 2026, and the city will have a real, weird, beautiful artifact of itself.

That's why we're building this.
